Forest Fires

Forest fires are commonly caused by the collection of "dead wood"
or debris in forests. The flamable material compounds on the floor of
the forest, making the process of combustion easier in extreme temperatures.

According to the California department of Forestry and Fire Protection, more than 6,000 fires have occured this year in California alone.

The latest forest fire in Northern California, the "Carr Fire", has reportedly burned down 700 homes and around 100,000 acres.

Some theorize that the recent icrease in forest fires is a result of government regulations stifling the domestic timber industry, resulting in less forest management such as the clearing of debris mentioned below.

One tactic to avoid larger fires is to engage in "controlled burning", where smaller fires are made to lower the total amount of flammable material in the long run.
